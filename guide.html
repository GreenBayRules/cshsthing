Multiple Choice

There are 74 multiple choice questions

How many questions do you need to get right to get a 5?

It is a lot lower than people think. If you were to get perfect scores on your Explore and Create Performance tasks, then you would only need a 64% or 47/74 on the multiple choice exam to get a 5. You can use this calculator to see what score you need for a 5.


What do I need to know?

If you take the class at your school and do well, you will do just fine on the multiple choice exam. The exam is very easy since you do not need a high score to get a 5. Here are a list of topics covered on the exam:

  • Big idea 1: Creativity
  • Big idea 2: Abstraction (~ 19%)
  • Big idea 3: Data and information (~ 18%)
  • Big idea 4: Algorithms (~ 20%)
  • Big idea 5: Programming (~ 20%)
  • Big idea 6: The internet (~13%)
  • Big idea 7: Global impact (~ 10%)

Tips:

Here are some general tips for the Multiple choice exam:

  • Select the right number of answers: There are two sections on the exam, in one you must choose 2 answers and in the other you must choose 1 answer. Make sure to remember which section you are so that you choose the right number of answers.
  • Keep track of time: Use a normal watch, not a smart watch. Once they call time, you must stop writing. If you keep filling in bubbles, your score will be disqualified. That's why it is important to know when you need to stop working and guess on the remaining questions.
  • Don't get discouraged: Remember, you don't need to get an A on the exam to get a good score. You can get a 64% on the exam and still get a 5 on the AP exam. Make sure to guess on every problem you don't know, and you will do fine.
